Daal Baati

    Ingredients

  • 1 cup wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup chana dal
  • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon urad dal


  • 1/4 cup green moong dal
  • 1/2 tablespoon ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 1/4 inch ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/4 cup toor daal

    Dough

  • 1/2 tablespoon semolina
  • 1 tablespoon ghee
  • 1 tablespoon ghee

Preparation method

  1. To prepare the baatis, take a glass bowl and add wheat flour with rawa, salt and ghee. Using your hands, knead a very stiff dough with warm water. Shape the dough in the size of a ping pong ball. Meanwhile, heat a gas tandoor and roast the dough balls on low heat for some time. Make sure they are brown and crusty.

  2. Then, break open from the top and pour some fresh ghee on the halves. Then to prepare the dal, wash all the dals together add 1 cup water and a quarter teaspoon of turmeric. Pressure cook the dals till 2 whistles. Allow the cooker to cool and remove the dal.

  3. Mix all the spice powders in 1/2 cup of water to make a thin paste. Put ghee in a pan over medium flame, add the cumin and coriander seeds. Once they splutter, add ginger. Then, add the paste of spice powders and fry for a minute, add the cooked dal.

  4. Then add the remaining water and stir well. Bring it to a boil. To get that extra zing, add lemon juice in it. Check and add salt if required. Garnish with chopped coriander. Serve hot with freshly prepared baatis and dal.
